Featured case study

Honda Cars Nepal

Honda Elevate
Launch Campaign

Content Strategy · Copywriting · Meta Ads

4–5 Months
~5 Cars sold via social leads

The Brief

Honda Cars Nepal was launching the Honda Elevate in Nepal - a completely new model entering the market of compact and mid-size SUV. The campaign needed to run across social media and paid ads for five months and do more than just create noise. It had to reach people who were genuinely in the market for an SUV and move them from curious to seriously considering.

The Approach

I led the campaign via content strategy, copywriting, and Meta Ads. The creative direction was build around positioning the Elevate for a specific kind of buyer rather than trying to speak to everyone. Ever post, ad, and caption had a job to make the right person stop, pay attention, and take the next step. The paid strategy was layrered on top of the organic content rather than running separately, so the two kept reinforcing each other throughout the campaign.

The Work

The Outcome

By the time the campaign wrapped, it had generated what Honda Cars Nepal's team described as an exceptional volume of hote leads through social media alone. Five Honda Elevate units were sold to buyers whose very first contact with the car came through a social media post or ad - which for a high-consideration purchase like a car, is a meaningful result.

A little
about me

Studying Computer Science and then ending up in content marketing is not as random as it sounds.

There's a certain kind of thinking that a CS degree drills into you. Breaking things down, finding the structure underneath, figuring out why something works before you try to make it work better. Turns out that's exactly what good content strategy requires. I just swapped out the code for words.

I've been at White Hat Digital for close to three years now, working across copywriting, content strategy, and brand storytelling for a range of brands. I spend a lot of my time thinking about how brands communicaate, the voice behind it, the intent behind it, and whether any of it is actually connecting with the people it's meant for.

I also get in front of the camera. I create and feature in short-form video content for TikTok and Instagram which has been one of the more interesting parts of the job. Video has a different kind of honesty to it. You can't hide behind good copy. Either the idea lands in the first few seconds, or it doesn't.

Outside of work, I'm usually travelling or reading about communication psychology, social norms, history and philosophy, watching how ideas spread, or thinking about why some stories stick and others disappear. It's the same curiosity that brought me to content in the first place.
